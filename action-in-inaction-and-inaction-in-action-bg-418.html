Action in Inaction & Inaction in Action (BG 4.18)

कर्मण्यकर्म यः पश्येदकर्मणि च कर्म यः। स बुद्धिमान् मनुष्येषु स युक्तः कृत्स्नकर्मकृत्।।

ahankAra is never free from karma & atma is ever free from karma. In the action which is falsely transferred on ātmā, upon that falsely transferred action, wise man sees inaction.  ( ātmani adhyastham-karma )

Actionlessness is two fold - 

1 - One is the absolute actionlessness which belongs to the ātmā. As far as the lay people are concerned the absolute actionlessness is not at all  nown because they don’t now the ātmā. As far as the lay people are concerned the absolute actionlessness is not at all  nown because they don’t  now the ātmā. 

2 - Then there is a second actionlessness which is the empirical actionlessness (it is anitya). Empirical akarma is as good as karma for all practical purposes. The empirical actionlessness  for  all  practical  purposes  can  be  equated  with karma because these two have got several common features, sādharmyam. Sādharmyam means common features.
Example - You never do the action of fasting, it is abstaining from eating and it is non-eating akarma which is presented as though another karma (when you say I did the fasting). 
akarma is a type of  arma only because of five reasons. 

- kAraka-sAdhyatvat
- prayatna-sAdhyatvat
- bahu-vidhatvAt
- punyapApa-janakatvAt
- chaudana vishayatvAt (shastric instructions)

kriyate iti karma. kriyate iti means vyApAra mAtram. 

Sarpē-rajjum paśyati means he sees rope in snake, in the place of snake a wise person sees the rope. Therefore karmaṇi means in the place of action wise  person  sees inaction.
