Chitrapata Nyaya

Seeker

"Chitrapata Nyaya" is an analogy used in Vedanta, to illustrate the concept of Maya, the illusion of the world.

In this analogy, the example given is of a cloth with various designs like mountains, sun, etc., woven into it. However, upon closer examination, one realizes that the cloth is made up only of threads arranged in a particular manner (long threads and cross threads). If one were to pull a single thread, the entire cloth would unravel, and what remains is just a ball of thread. The mountains, sun, and other designs were never inherent in the cloth itself; they were merely woven into it. In reality, only the threads exist.

Similarly, according to this analogy, the world as we perceive it—earth, sky, mountains, sun, and all other objects—is like the designs on the cloth. These are merely names and forms woven into the fabric of consciousness. When we delve deeper into the nature of reality, we realize that these forms are not inherent in the underlying substrate but are superimposed upon it. Just as pulling a thread reveals the true nature of the cloth, the realization of the underlying consciousness unveils the true nature of reality, where names and forms dissolve, leaving only the essence of existence.
