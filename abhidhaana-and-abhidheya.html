Abhidhaana & Abhidheya

In Vedanta, the terms "abhidhaana" and "abhidheya" refer to fundamental concepts concerning language and its relationship to reality.

Abhidhaana (the word): Abhidhaana represents the word itself, the linguistic sign or symbol that is used to convey meaning. It's the spoken or written form of language, such as individual words, phrases, or sentences. Abhidhaana is the medium through which communication takes place, enabling us to express ideas, concepts, and experiences.

Abhidheya (the object revealed by the word): Abhidheya refers to the object or meaning conveyed by the word. It's what the word signifies or represents in the mind of the speaker or listener. When we use language, the intention is to convey certain meanings or concepts through words. These meanings or concepts are the abhidheya. They can range from concrete objects and qualities to abstract ideas and philosophical principles.
