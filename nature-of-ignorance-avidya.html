Vedanta

Nature of Ignorance / Avidya

ज्ञानेन तु तदज्ञानं येषां नाशितमात्मनः। तेषामादित्यवज्ज्ञानं प्रकाशयति तत्परम् ।।5.16।।

Ignorance, or "agyaan," isn't simply the absence of knowledge but rather its adversary. It persists until displaced by knowledge. Agyaan shouldn't be misconstrued as a lack of knowledge because mere absence ("abhaava") doesn't lead to issues. Problems arise from presence, or "bhaava," not absence. Thus, agyaan isn't the absence of knowledge, or "gyaan abhaava." Ignorance opposes knowledge and, being problematic, holds significance as long as it persists. However, in the presence of knowledge, it ceases to exist.

Similarly to how darkness dissipates in the presence of light, ignorance vanishes in the presence of knowledge. Ignorance has a tangible existence, or "bhaava," causing errors and troubles. For instance, mistaking a rope for a snake due to ignorance induces fear. Likewise, in dreams, ignorance about the true self leads to perplexing scenarios, such as a millionaire dreaming of being a beggar and vice versa.

Ignorance is dispelled by knowledge specific to the subject. Just as knowledge about a pot dispels ignorance about it, understanding the true nature of the self eradicates self-ignorance.

This realization occurs in the minds of those previously ignorant. Krishna likens this transformative knowledge to the rising sun. Just as the sun dispels darkness and illuminates everything around, self-knowledge eradicates self-ignorance and reveals the self as Brahman—a truth previously unknown.

Understanding the analogy between knowledge and the sun is crucial. Knowledge doesn't illuminate the self for viewing; rather, it dispels ignorance and unveils the inherent truth—the self itself is the light. This knowledge, known as vritti-gyaana, arises as the thought "I am Brahman - aham brahma asmi" in the mind. This thought signifies the knowledge that eradicates self-ignorance, revealing the truth about the self being Brahman. "I am Brahman" represents the ultimate truth, or tattva, recognized through teachings from scripture.
