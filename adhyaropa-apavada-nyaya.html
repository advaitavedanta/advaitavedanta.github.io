adhyAropa apavAda nyAya

Seeker

adhyAropa refers to the declaration of real differences among the creator, the creation, and the individual selves.

apavAda , on the other hand, involves the negation of these differences.

Initially, the teaching emphasizes duality, acknowledging the everyday experience of separateness. This stage is akin to teaching a child the alphabet before introducing complex literature.

As the seeker progresses, the scripture gradually reveals that the individual selves are not separate entities from the divine but are inseparable parts of it. This process of negation begins even without the seeker consciously realizing it.

During these stages, the scripture encourages the pursuit of righteousness, wealth, and desires, as mentioned in yesterday's discussion.

Once the student has developed strong reasoning skills, their mind becomes receptive like fertile soil awaiting seeds. Spiritual knowledge (Sravana, Manana, and Nididhyasana) is present in significant measure.

At this juncture, the scripture begins guiding the seeker towards the teachings of liberation. The examination of the worlds becomes increasingly relevant, leading to the gradual revelation of the meaning of the mahAvAkyAs. These teachings unveil the truth that there is no creator other than the Self, no creation other than the Self, and no individual selves other than the Self.

Vedanta expounds the Mahavakyas through various methods, such as the Eka vignyAnEna sarva vignyAna prakriyA by chAndogya , the Pancha Kosha (five sheaths) prakriya in the Taittiriya Upanishad, and the kArya-kArana (cause-effect) prakriya found in many Upanishads. The pinnacle of these teachings lies in the Mandukya Upanishad's exposition of the avastha traya prakriya and the shariira traya prakriya.

All of these teachings rely on the prakriya of adhyAropa-apavAda, sometimes referred to as the method of superimposition and negation (adhyAropa apavAda nyAya)

Indeed, this method represents the aspiration of every teacher and Upanishad—to guide seekers from the realm of apparent duality to the realization of ultimate non-duality.
