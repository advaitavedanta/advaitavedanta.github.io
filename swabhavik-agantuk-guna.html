Swabhavik / Agantuk Guna

Seeker

The concept of "guna" refers to attributes or qualities that are inherent in objects or beings. These gunas can be categorized into two main types:

  1. Swabhavik Guna (Intrinsic Properties):

    • Swabhavik guna refers to inherent or intrinsic properties that are natural or essential to an object's existence.
    • These qualities are fundamental to the nature or essence of the object and are not dependent on external factors.
    • Swabhavik gunas are considered to be enduring and permanent characteristics of an object.
    • For example, qualities like hardness in a diamond, liquidity in water, or transparency in glass are considered swabhavik gunas because they are inherent to these substances and remain consistent under normal conditions.

  2. Agantuk Guna (Transient or Acquired Properties):

    • Agantuk guna refers to temporary or acquired properties that are not intrinsic to the object's nature but are acquired due to external influences or conditions.
    • These qualities are not inherent to the object and can change or disappear under different circumstances.
    • Agantuk gunas are often dependent on external factors or interactions with other objects.
    • For example, the hotness of water when heated by fire is an agantuk guna because it is a temporary property acquired due to the influence of fire. Once the water cools down, its hotness diminishes, indicating the transient nature of this property.
