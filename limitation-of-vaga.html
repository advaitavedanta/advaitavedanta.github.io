Limitation of Vaga


Limitation of words, or "Vaga," refers to the constraints of language in expressing certain aspects of reality. Words are powerful tools for communication, but they have their limitations. Adi Shankara says words can only reveal four specific aspects:

Jaati: This refers to the classification or category of a thing. Words can label or classify objects based on their type or category. For example, words can distinguish between different species of animals or types of objects.
Guna: Guna refers to the qualities or attributes of a thing. Words can describe the characteristics or properties of objects, such as their color, size, shape, or texture. By using words, we can convey information about the qualities of things.
Kriya: Kriya pertains to the actions or activities associated with a thing. Words can describe the actions performed by objects or individuals. They can convey information about what something does or how it behaves.
Sambandha: Sambandha refers to the relationship or connection between things. Words can describe the relationships between objects or entities, such as cause and effect, association, or dependency. They can convey information about how things are related to each other.
"sangya," which refers to the naming or identification of objects, is closely related to "jaati" (classification), as it involves categorizing objects by assigning them names or labels. By naming objects, we establish their identity and distinguish them from others, which is an essential function of language.
